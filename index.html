Our Story

Every Flavor Tells a Story

Our inspiration to this project is to promote the different local food in the philippines and its authenticity. This proves that philippines provide good food that can cater your different taste. This wide variety of dishes that we offer to you tells different story and history depending on which city the dish you order came from. Hopefully that their story and dish satisfy your taste.

Specialties

Pampanga Cuisine | Ilocos Cuisine

  • Sisig

    Special Php 400.00

    A dish made from pork jowl and ears (maskara), pork belly, and chicken liver

  • Empanada

    Special Php 200.00

    A rice or galapong wrapper stuffed with shredded green papaya

  • Betute

    Php 500.00

    An exotic specialty of Pampanga of deep-fried rice field frogs filled with minced pork

  • Bagnet

    Php 250.00

    A dish consisting of pork belly boiled and deep fried until it is crispy

  • Morcon

    Php 300.00

    A braised beef roulade made with beef flank steak stuffed with hard-boiled eggs, carrots, pickled cucumber, cheese, and various sausages

  • Papaitan

    Php 500.00

    A Filipino-Ilocano stew made with goat meat and offal and flavored with its bile, chyme, or cud

  • Kamaru

    Php 200.00

    A dish where the main ingredient, chicken or pork, is substituted with crickets

  • Pinakbet

    Php 600.00

    An indigenous Filipino dish that is a mix of different kinds of vegetables

Bicol Cuisine | Cebu Cuisine

  • Bicol Express

    Special Php 600.00

    A stew made from long chili peppers or small chili peppers , coconut milk/coconut cream , shrimp paste or stockfish, onion, pork, ginger and garlic

  • Lechon Cebu

    Special Php 1200.00

    A young pig stuffed with a secret mix of herbs and spices (usually including star anise, spring onions, lemongrass), skewered on a bamboo pole and roasted whole over hot coals

  • Kinalas

    Php 300.00

    A dish consisting of noodles (pancit) garnished by scraped meat from pork or beef's head and other parts, enhanced with a thick deep-brown sauce coming from the brains of a cow or pig

  • Balbacua

    Php 250.00

    A beef stew made from beef, collagen-rich beef parts (oxtail, skin, and joints), and various spices cooked for several hours until very tender.

  • Kinunot

    Php 200.00

    Avocados with crab meat, red onion, crab salad stuffed red bell pepper and green bell pepper.

  • Pancit Bam-i

    Php 500.00

    A noodle recipe that is a combination of pancit canton and sotanghon noodles along with different vegetables and meat dishes

  • Laing

    Php 350.00

    A dish of shredded or whole taro leaves with meat or seafood cooked in thick coconut milk spiced with labuyo chili, lemongrass, garlic, shallots, ginger, and shrimp paste

  • Puso

    Php 200.00

    Vegetables, cheeses, ground meats, tomato sauce, seasonings and spices
